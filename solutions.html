Build Paths

How universities usually expand beyond the built-in agents

Once admissions and retention are in place, the next decision is usually whether internal teams want to build the next workflow or whether the institution would rather use Professional Services for planning, integration, and rollout support.

Build on the platform

Universities that want internal control can use EduLab to build additional agents for financial aid, registration, billing, holds, surveys, advancement, and other repetitive outreach programs.

  • Best for institutions with strong internal ownership of workflow design
  • Useful when the team wants to extend the platform incrementally
  • Good fit for departments that can manage agent content and operational follow-through

Build with Professional Services

Institutions often use Professional Services when the next workflow touches multiple systems, requires deeper planning, or needs a clearer operational ownership model before launch.

  • Best for cross-office workflows with integration and reporting requirements
  • Useful when rollout needs partner-led discovery, implementation sequencing, or staff handoff design
  • Good fit when admissions, IT, and operations teams need a shared implementation plan
