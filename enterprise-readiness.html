University Fit

What EduLab is designed to fit around

EduLab is not positioned as a stand-alone experiment. It is built to support real university outreach, routing, and reporting workflows across the teams already responsible for student communication and follow-through.

Communications stack

Support outreach across email and SMS while keeping the student conversation tied to the workflow the institution is trying to improve.

CRM and SIS context

Use student and operational context where it matters so staff are not responding without the surrounding picture.

Reporting and analytics

Give leadership and operational teams usable visibility into response patterns, friction themes, and intervention signals.

Staff handoff

Escalate to human teams with context, not anonymous records, so support can continue without restarting the conversation.

Compliance and Deployment

Deployment options designed for university governance requirements

EduLab is built on Google Cloud Platform using managed services that provide a strong foundation for security, availability, and compliance-conscious deployment. Additional application, operational, and governance controls are implemented by EduLab to address customer security requirements, while institutions can choose the deployment model that best fits their governance and regulatory posture.

Dedicated tenant environments

Each customer is provisioned in a dedicated tenant environment with logical separation across data, configurations, and workloads.

Customer-managed GCP option

For universities with existing GCP investments, EduLab can be deployed within customer-managed GCP environments so the institution can retain governance, security controls, and operational oversight inside its own cloud ecosystem.

Independently assessed cloud foundation

EduLab is built on Google Cloud services that have undergone independent third-party security and compliance assessments, including SOC 1, SOC 2, SOC 3, and ISO 27001.

Assured Workloads option

For organizations requiring enhanced compliance, data residency, or governance controls, EduLab can be deployed within a Google Cloud Assured Workloads environment, providing additional compliance guardrails and policy enforcement capabilities as an optional add-on service.

Data footprint for most use cases

Most EduLab use cases begin with a small operational data footprint. In many deployments, the initial workflow only needs directory-level information such as first name, email address, and phone number.

That gives institutions a way to start with targeted student engagement workflows without requiring unnecessary data exposure at the outset.

Rollout Model

Three ways universities can move forward

The core decision is not whether to start from zero. It is whether the institution begins with built-in agents, builds additional workflows internally, or brings in Professional Services for more complex rollout needs.

Start with built-in agents

Use the built-in admissions and retention agents when the immediate goal is website conversion, melt visibility, or early persistence support.

Add workflows internally

Extend the platform into workflows like financial aid, billing, registration, surveys, or holds when the institution wants to develop those agents internally.

Use Professional Services

Use Professional Services when additional agents require integration planning, workflow design, partner-led rollout, or broader operational coordination.
