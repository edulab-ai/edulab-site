Towson University Program Snapshot

Towson University case study: student outreach, response rates, and enrollment signals

Towson used EduLab in two parts of the student journey: early intervention for at-risk students and enrollment outreach for students who had not yet enrolled. For enrollment management leaders, the value was stronger response and better visibility into student barriers. For CIOs and business applications teams, the value was a clearer operating model for outreach, routing, and reporting.

The challenge

  • Students were not responding consistently to outreach.
  • Important questions and hesitation signals were hard to surface at scale.
  • Staff time alone was not enough to keep every conversation moving.

Deployment approach

  • Use personalized AI conversations to engage students across key moments.
  • Guide next steps instead of relying on static reminders or generic follow-up.
  • Escalate to staff when human support is needed, with context already captured.
Program Breakdown

The Towson program included two distinct efforts

Looking at these efforts separately helps enrollment leaders review the student-outcome side of the work and helps CIO and business applications teams review how each workflow supported outreach, routing, and reporting.

Early Intervention Effort

Focused on identifying at-risk students earlier and confirming when students were actively struggling.

  • 21,557 students analyzed
  • 409 at-risk students detected
  • 90 students confirmed they were struggling
  • 17 students enrolled

Enrollment Outreach Campaign

Focused on re-engaging students who had not yet enrolled and surfacing barriers at scale.

  • 12,834 appointment reminders sent
  • 3,161 non-enrolled students contacted
  • 288 students confirmed they would enroll
  • 521 students reported difficulties or no plan to enroll
  • 160 students enrolled
Why it mattered

What the results meant for university teams

Stronger student response

Questions could be addressed while intent was still active, instead of after momentum had already faded.

Less manual chasing

Teams were not asked to replace every manual touch, only to step in where judgment and relationship mattered most.

Better operating signals

Conversation data made it easier to see where students hesitated, what they needed, and where outreach could improve.
